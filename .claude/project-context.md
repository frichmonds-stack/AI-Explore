# Project Context

## Purpose
An AI literacy platform for K-12 teachers. Helps non-technical educators understand what AI is, what it can't do, its risks to children, and how to apply it responsibly.

Read-and-explore resource — no quizzes, no progress gates, no interactivity. Teachers browse at their own pace.

## Audience
- K-12 classroom teachers, no technical background
- CPD (continuing professional development) mindset
- Time-poor: content must be scannable and clearly structured

## Content Philosophy
- Balanced and fair — AI neither hyped nor demonised
- Risks first, not last — child development concerns surfaced throughout, not buried
- Evidence-based — references Willingham (cognitive load), Vygotsky (social learning), Deci & Ryan (motivation)
- Pedagogically grounded — every AI application filtered through "does this serve learning?"
- Plain language — no jargon, no code

## Content Architecture
1. **AI Foundations** — what AI is, how it works, capabilities, limitations, hallucination/bias/privacy
2. **Risks & Responsible Use** — cognitive risks (critical thinking, memory), social risks (empathy, identity), privacy, equity, ethics
3. **AI in Your Teaching Practice** — admin, classroom, curriculum, assessment
4. **Teaching Pedagogies + AI** — constructivism, Socratic method, UDL; where AI supports vs. undermines pedagogy
5. **Further Exploration** — tool guides (ChatGPT, Copilot, NotebookLM), curated research

## Planned but Not Yet Built
- Backend API (currently all content is static JSON)
- School policy section (explicitly deferred)
- User accounts / personalisation
- Search across content
